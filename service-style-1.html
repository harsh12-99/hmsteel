  • Mobile Welding

    In Mobile welding we perform services at various locations, typically not limited to a fixed workshop. We have the equipment and capability to travel to different sites to perform welding work, whether it's for construction, repairs, or other welding needs. This flexibility allows us to work on projects that may not be easily transported to a traditional welding shop.

  • Steel Fabrication

    "Steel fabrication" is a process in which we shape, cut, and assemble steel materials to create a final product or structure. It involves various techniques such as cutting, welding, bending, and machining to transform raw steel materials into specific components or finished products. Steel fabrication is a crucial part of many industries, including construction, manufacturing, automotive, aerospace, and more.

  • Sliding Door

    A "sliding door" is a type of door that moves horizontally along a track or rails to open and close. Unlike traditional hinged doors that swing open, sliding doors operate by sliding sideways, typically parallel to a wall or another door panel. They are often used in various architectural and interior design applications due to their space-saving and aesthetic advantages.

  • Workbench

    A "workbench" is a sturdy table or bench where we design it for various tasks, particularly those involving manual work, assembly, repair, or craftsmanship. Workbenches typically have a flat and durable surface, often made of wood, metal, or another robust material, that can withstand the demands of specific tasks.

  • PRECAST CONNECTION WELDING

    A "precast connection weld" refers to the welding process used in connecting precast concrete elements or components together. Precast concrete is a construction technique where concrete elements, such as beams, columns, panels, and slabs, are cast in a controlled environment (typically a factory) before being transported to a construction site and assembled to create a building or structure.
